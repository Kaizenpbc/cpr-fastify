## 2. SCOPE OF SERVICE

2.1 **Grant of Access.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Provider grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable right to access and use the Service during the Term for the Customer's internal business purposes.

2.2 **Service Description.** The Service is a multi-tenant, hosted SaaS platform that provides the Customer with the following capabilities:

   (a) Course scheduling and management for CPR, first aid, and related training programs;

   (b) Student enrollment, roster management, and attendance tracking;

   (c) Instructor assignment and management;

   (d) Certification issuance, tracking, and expiry management;

   (e) Billing, invoicing, and payment tracking;

   (f) Organizational reporting and analytics; and

   (g) Such other features as may be made available by the Provider from time to time.

2.3 **Hosting.** The Service is hosted on infrastructure provided by TMD Hosting on Canadian servers. The Provider is solely responsible for the operation, maintenance, and security of the Service.

2.4 **Authorized Users.** The Customer may permit its Authorized Users to access the Service. The Customer is responsible for all acts and omissions of its Authorized Users and for ensuring that each Authorized User complies with the terms of this Agreement.

2.5 **Support.** The Provider shall provide reasonable technical support to the Customer via email during regular business hours (Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM Eastern Time, excluding statutory holidays).

## 4. SERVICE LEVEL

4.1 **Uptime Target.** The Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain Service availability of ninety-nine point five percent (99.5%) measured on a monthly basis, excluding Scheduled Maintenance.

4.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** The Provider may perform scheduled maintenance outside of regular business hours. The Provider shall use reasonable efforts to provide the Customer with at least twenty-four (24) hours' advance notice of any scheduled maintenance that may affect Service availability.

4.3 **Monitoring.** The Provider monitors Service availability using UptimeRobot (or an equivalent monitoring service) with checks at five (5) minute intervals on the Service health endpoint.

4.4 **Exclusions.** The uptime target in Section 4.1 does not apply to service interruptions caused by:

   (a) Scheduled maintenance;

   (b) Force Majeure events as described in Section 16;

   (c) The Customer's equipment, software, or network connections;

   (d) The Customer's or its Authorized Users' actions or omissions; or

   (e) Third-party services or infrastructure outside the Provider's reasonable control.

4.5 **No SLA Credits.** The uptime target in Section 4.1 is a target and not a guarantee. The Provider does not offer service level credits at this time.

8.2 **Account Deactivation.** After the expiration of the Export Window, the Provider shall deactivate all Authorized User accounts associated with the Customer in accordance with the Provider's offboarding procedures.

8.3 **Data Retention.** Following deactivation, Customer Data shall be retained and handled in accordance with Section 10 (Data Retention) of this Agreement and the Provider's privacy policy.

8.4 **Accrued Obligations.** Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any obligations that accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including the obligation to pay all Fees due.

8.5 **Survival.** Sections 1 (Definitions), 5 (Fees and Payment, to the extent of accrued obligations), 8 (Effect of Termination), 9 (Customer Data and Privacy), 10 (Data Retention), 11 (Confidentiality), 12 (Intellectual Property), 13 (Warranties and Disclaimers), 15 (Limitation of Liability), and 18 (Governing Law and Dispute Resolution) shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement.
